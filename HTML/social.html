  • Family:-


    •                   A joint family in India is where the entire family all live together, which can include parents, wife, children and occasionally relatives. At the head of the family is a ‚ÄòKarta‚Äô, a senior male or female who makes economic and social decisions on behalf of the entire family, and other relations can be equal, of mutual respect or teasing in nature. Income goes into a common pool, which benefits all members. Nowadays, economic development and urbanisation have led to an increase in nuclear-like families than joint families. Arranged marriage is still a strong tradition in India. This dates back to Vedic times in 1500‚Äì1100 BCE where suitable matches from around the kingdom would compete in competitions to win the hand of a royal bride. Here are some aspects of Indian family culture :


  • Structure: Indian families are often multigenerational, consisting of three to four generations living together in the same household. These families often share a kitchen and purse.

  • Patriarchal structure: In patriarchal families, male members, such as fathers, husbands, and elder brothers, are responsible for making decisions for the rest of the family.

  • Extended family: Extended family and kinship are important aspects of Indian family systems.

  • Respect for elder: It is considered the duty of every son and daughter to respect their elders, especially to take care of their parents in their old age.

  • Family history: Family history can be used as a source of inspiration, finding strength in the struggles and successes of forefathers.

  • Values: Values such as personal integrity, tolerance for diversity and differences, social responsibility, respect for persons, and sense of justice are important in family life education

  • Temples:-


    •                   Exploring Indian temples is a magical experience, but there are a few things to remember before you visit. Many of these sacred buildings were deliberately built at places rich in positive energy from the magnetic wave lines of the Earth. Most temples feature one main idol, which has a copper plate called the Garbhagriha or Moolasthan underneath that absorbs and resonates this underground energy. One of the customs and traditions in Indian culture is to have a bath or shower before entering a temple or at least wash your hands and feet to cleanse yourself of negative thoughts and evil influences. Appropriate attire is really important, namely conservative clothing to indicate respect. Women should ideally wear a modest top and a calf or ankle-length skirt or trousers that will allow sitting comfortably cross-legged on the floor. Men should wear trousers and a shirt. Avoid wearing leather or animal skin of any kind, as this is offensive to practising Hindus. You will also need to remove your footwear before entering places of worship to prevent any dirt from coming into a cleansed and sanctified environment. A top tip is to choose shoes that are easy to remove. If you‚Äôd prefer to keep your socks on, that‚Äôs fine: just make sure they are clean and free from holes! Temples are a symbolic house, the seat of Hindu gods. Temples are built to honor and worship various deities through sculptures, idols, and other art forms. Temples are a manifestation of the entire cosmos, representing the earthly world, the heavenly world, the astral world, and the world below. Temples are sacred spaces where devotees can connect with the divine through prayer, meditation, and other forms of worship. The customs and etiquette for visiting temples varies across India. Devotees in major temples may bring in symbolic offerings for the puja. Temples are the focus for all aspects of everyday life in the Hindu community, including religious, cultural, educational, and social.

  • Literature:-


    •                   India has many great epics dating back many centuries in the form of stories, poems, plays and self-help guides. The two most famous Hindu epics are the Ramayana and Mahabharata, which both contain thrilling tales of gods and demons, love and war and chariots and kidnappings. These stories have been told and retold for thousands of years and play a huge part in Indian culture. The Ramayana tells the story of Rama, prince of the legendary kingdom of Kosala, and follows his fourteen-year exile to the forest urged by his father King Dasharatha. The Mahabharata is the longest poem that has been written in Sanskrit. Both epics tell of good triumphing over evil and show the values of devotion, loyalty, sacrifice and truth. Indian literature is a broad term that refers to writings produced in the Indian subcontinent, including works created before and after the formation of the Republic of India in 1947. It encompasses a variety of literary forms, including: Epics Songs Dramatic and didactic poetry Narrative and scientific writing Oral poetry and music Indian literature is considered one of the oldest and richest literatures in the world. The oldest works include oral transmission of knowledge. In ancient India, literature was written in verses, and poetic form was the standard form of writing. The Mahabharata has more than 100,000 verses, and the Ramayana has over 24,000. The Mantras (500 BCE), sacred books, were also written in poetic form and are read during times of worship. Some examples of Indian literature include: Tamil Sangam literature This literature is known for its poetic nature, philosophical texts, and secular vision of the writers. It spans over more than 2000 years and includes the social, economical, and political aspects of Tamil history. Mulk Raj Anand Anands novels, particularly ‚ÄúUntouchable‚Äù (1935), were groundbreaking.
